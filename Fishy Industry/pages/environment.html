Garbage Patch

The Great Pacific garbage patch is approximately 1.6 million square kilometers long and garbage truck loads of garbage are dumped into the ocean every minute. Plastic in the ocean slowly breaks down into small microplastics which seeps into every living creature in the ocean. Those fish get eaten by other fish or get caught and eaten by us. We are eating the plastics that we dump into the ocean. Plastic is definitely a huge problem in the ocean. It is such a big problem that it is worse than the worst oil spill ever recorded. The worst oil spill occured in the Gulf of Mexico. That oil spill killed a lot of marine animals but guess what, one day of fishing in the Gulf of Mexico killed more fish than the oil spill did in one month. Marine life in that area actually benefited from the oil spill because there was a lot less fishing going on in the contaminated waters. Although plastic in the ocean seems like a massive problem that needs to be fixed right away, even if not a single piece of garbage ends up in the ocean from today onwards, we will still be ripping the ecosystem apart due to commercial fishing. In addition, more than ⅓ of the plastic in the ocean is fishing nets or fishing gear. The most talked about plastic which is plastic straws, make up less than 0.03% of the plastics in the ocean. Why are we so focused on reducing plastic straw usage when fishing nets and gear make up more of the plastic than straws?

Trawling

Trawling is by far the most destructive form of fishing. Trawling is like the deforestation of the ocean. Trawling is a method of fishing where a giant net is being pulled across the water by boats. These nets drag heavy weights onto the bottom of the ocean, scaring the sea floor and leaving a barren wasteland behind. The largest trawls are so huge, they can swallow up to 13 jumbo jet planes. To imagine how destructive trawling is, every year, approximately 25 million acres of forest are destroyed from bulldozing which is equivalent to losing 27 soccer fields of forest every minute. Bottom trawling wipes out 3.9 billion acres every year which is equivalent to losing 4,300 soccer fields very single minute. That is really mind boggling. This is why coral reefs around our coasts are all gone.

Climate Change

Overfishing has big impacts on our climate. It might be what you are thinking but the animals that move up and down to move across water help the ocean absorb the heat from the atmosphere. As animals move, warmer water comes down to mix with the colder water below. The power of animals moving up and down is as great as all the wind, waves, tides and currents in the world combined. Life in the ocean is crucial for holding onto carbon and preventing it from being released to the atmosphere. Animals take in carbon and when they die, the carbon sinks down to the bottom of the ocean essentially making the ocean a large carbon sink. 90% of the world’s carbon is stored in the ocean and per acre, coastal plants store 20x more carbon than forests. Although planting trees and slowing down deforestation can help with climate change, ultimately, saving the ocean will play a bigger role in addressing climate change. To address climate change you have to protect the ocean and to protect the ocean, simply leave it alone. A great thing about the marine ecosystem is that it can easily bounce back to normal if we let it happen.
