Whales

Whales are vital for the health of the ocean ecosystem. They help fertilize marine plants called phytoplankton when they excrete waste. These phytoplankton absorb 4x more carbon dioxide than the Amazon rainforest. There have been laws to protect whales like the international whaling ban since 1986 but there are numerous countries whaling illegally. Most notorious of them is Japan. They have been rounding whales and dolphins secretly into coves illegally. The whaling site is so protected that authorities spy on your activities right when you enter the location. They are able to do all this with funding from the marine park entertainment industry. Young, wild dolphins and whales are expensive so catched dolphins and whales are sold to the industry. However, for every one dolphin captured, 12 were killed. Why are the fishermans killing more dolphins than capturing them?

Dolphins

Just like whales, dolphins also help fertilize phytoplankton when they excrete waste. They are vital in the ecosystem. Unfortunately, just like whales, they are killed by commercial fishing. They are either killed by bycatch or intentionally for other reasons. One reasoning was to control fish populations. They were killing dolphins so that there were more fish in the ocean for fishermans to catch. However, killing dolphins was just a scapegoat to continue Japanâ€™s 40 million dollar bluefin tuna industry. The bluefin industry was using dolphins as their reasoning for the fish stock dropping. Bluefins are rare and expensive but there is a high demand. Due to the bluefin industry, less than 3% of pacific bluefin tuna remain from decades ago.

Sea Turtles

Although, there are no fisheries that target sea turtles their populations are still plummeting. 6 out of the 7 sea turtle species are endangered due to bycatch. Approximately 1,000 sea turtles are killed by plastic per year. That is nothing compared to how many sea turtles are killed in the US per year. In the US alone, 250,000 turtles are killed per year.

Sharks

Not only does Japan kill dolphins, whales, and bluefin tuna, they are killing sharks too. Japan has a shark finning industry that captures sharks and cuts off their fins to export to China. After cutting their fins, they are thrown back to the ocean but the majority already died before returning back to the ocean. Shark fin soup is a status symbol in China. They are only a status symbol. Shark fins have no nutritional value and they have little to no taste. Sharks play a massive role in the marine ecosystem. Sharks are on the very top of the food chain or we can call them level 1 animals. Level 1 animals eat level 2 animals. Without level 1 animals, level 2 animals will have no predators and over populate quickly. Level 2 will eat all the level 3 fish and die out. This chain reaction will continue to the organisms on the bottom of the food chain. Without sharks, the ocean will be an empty wasteland, and without a thriving ocean, we will all die.
