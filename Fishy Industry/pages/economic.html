Sustainable Fishing

Commercial fishing is essentially wildlife poaching on a mass scale. Up to 2.7 trillion fish are caught every year. Global fish populations are plummeting to near extinction and if we continue on this trend, the ocean will be empty by 2048. We need a quick solution. There has been a phrase spreading around the internet which is sustainable fishing. Well, there is no such thing as sustainable fishing. Sustainable fishing is just a marketing phrase to get people supporting the corporation. Corporations will not come out against hunting, fishing and meat eating because they fear the loss of membership support. Oceana is the largest marine conservation group. In their website, there is no mention of reducing or eliminating seafood consumption anywhere. In addition, they recommend the best way to save fish is to eat fish.

Subsidies

Overfishing has caused gobal fish populations to fall. When there are less fish, fish becomes more valuable but why arn't their prices inscreasing substantially? Well, it's because we subsidies them. Subsidy is taxpayer money that is given to an industry to keep the price of a product or service artificially low. One of these subsidies is the fishing industry. Governments go to extreme measures to prevent people from exposing the economic activities that they subsist on at sea. If they do get exposed, corporations might lose all their money, fishermans will lose their jobs, and the whole industry might fall apart. There is an increasing number of countries that are pouring more money to catch fish than the value of fish coming back in. So even though you don’t eat fish, you are still sustaining fisheries because you are paying it in your taxes. Your taxes go to the government to subsidise fishing, to keep the fishing industry afloat (pun unintended) and allow you to purchase seafood at a low price. Imagine just saying that. How corrupted does that sound? Subsidies originally started as a means to ensure food security by having fish prices so low that anyone can afford them. Now subsidies are the cause of food insecurity in many developing nations.

Food Insecurity

It’s easy to guess that overfishing will result in food insecurities especially for developing nations that heavily relies on seafood for food. Once your area of water no longer has fish inside, people will go far and wide to look for fish. And one place with an abundance of fish and the only places with a thriving ecosystem left is the continent Africa. Fishing by the European Union in places like West Africa is driven by European Union subsidies. Large efficient fishing vessels fishing around African waters leaves local fishing businesses unprofitable and without fish. One of the causes of the infamous pirates of Somalia that are feared across the world were actually once fishermans working to feed their families. When Somalia fell for civil war, foreign illegal fishing vessels invaded their waters and began taking their fish, which is effectively pulling food from Somalians' mouths. This is giving Somali fishermen no choice but to move into another line of work which is piracy. Without seafood, people had to hunt wildlife for food. This does not only have devastating impacts on life on land but wildlife carry diseases like ebola that can spread and lead to an epidemic. The theft of fish stock in Africa is causing ebola outbreaks in West Africa.

Forced Labor

Surprisingly, there is forced labor in the fishing industry. In such an ordinary industry, there is forced labor. Most notable is Thailand, where more than 50,000 fisheries fish under the Thai flag. There was so much competition for fish that fishermens needed to find a way to fish cheaply due to the lack of fish being caught. They don't have efficent ships or technology to reduce the cost of fishing so free and cheap labor is their only option. Slaves were abused and harmed in ships and even thrown off ships if they misbehaved. The government even has authorities to help cover up the illegal activities and prevent exposure.
